Austrian Desserts

Topfenstrudel

Php 160 (per serving)

A baked dessert composed of sweetened quark cheese folded in puff pastry. It is then topped with rasperries to balance out the flavors.

Salzburger Nockerl

Php 140 (per serving)

Considered as "vanilla-flavored dumplings", the Salzburger Nockerl is Knowing for being a souffle-like pastry. Powdered sugar is then dusted on top of it for extra sweetness. It was named after the city of Salzburg, where it originated from.

Linzer Torte

Php 180 (per slice)

A dessert named after the city of Linz. It is a pie-like pastry with a buttery crust that is brought out with lemon juice, cinnamon, and hazelnuts. The filling for ours is rasperry jam, deviating from the usual redcurrant jam.

Mozartkugel

Php 100 (for 5 pieces)

As a type of chocolate confection named after a certain composer, Mozartkugel was dedicated to Mozart. It is made of a pistachio-flavored marzipan ball coated in nougat, then finished off with a thick layer of dark chocolate.

Vanillekipferl

Php 120 (for 6 pieces)

These are crescent-shaped vanilla cookies dusted with powdered sugar containing walnuts. They were made after Austria's victory after a war against Turkey.

Kaiserschmarrn

Php 110 (per serving)

Also known as "The Emperor's Mess", Kaiserschmarrn was named after the Emperor Kaiser Franz Joseph, for it was his favorite dessert. It consists of scrambled pancakes, berry preserve, and a dusting of powdered sugar.

Apfelstrudel

Php 140 (per serving)

One of Austria's most famous desserts, the Apfelstrudel is a puff pastry filled with apple filling. Like an apple pie of some sort, but with raisins.

Krapfen

Php 120 (For three)

A European-style donut filled with jam and topped with powdered sugar. It is best paired with good old plain coffee.

Cremeschnitte

Php 200

A puff pastry dessert filled with a thick layer of custard topped with raspberries for extra flair.

Sachertorte

Php 170 (per slice)

A famous Austrian chocolate cake with chocolate cake layers coated with a thin layer of apricot jam, frosted with chocolate icing, then served with a generous dollop of whipped cream on the side.

Marillenkuchen

Php 190 (per slice)

A light lemon-flavored sponge cake with embedded apricots in the center of it, lightly dusted with powdered sugar.

Gugelhupf

Php 120 (per slice)

Known as the "Bundt Cake" in English, it is famous for its donut shape. The shape makes it bake more evenly in the oven, making it a moist cake even without frosting to fill it.

Palatschinken

Php 100

The Austrian rendition of the French crepe, but slightly thicker. It is filled with apple cinnamon, has a side of strawberries, and is dusted with powdered sugar.

Topfenknodel

Php 200 (for 10 pieces)

A savory dumpling-like dessert dubbed as "Austrian curd dumplings". Its taste is similar to that of a donut and is served with raspberries on the side.

French Pastries

Choux au Craquelin

Php 200 (for 5 pieces)

A French Choux pastry with a cookie layer called a "Craquelin" on top. It is filled with diplomat cream and is served cold.

Croissant

Php 150 (per piece)

A buttery pastry with several layers of flakey dough shaped into a crescent.

Chouquette

Php 150 (for 10 pieces)

Bite-sized treats made out of Choux pastry with pearl sugar studs on the exterior of its shell.

Mille Feuille

Php 220

A pastry named after Napoleon and has "a thousand layers" due to the fact that it is made of puff pastry. It is then filled with pastry cream and strawberries sandwiched inbetween.

Paris-Brest

Php 240

A dessert comprised of a ring of piped Choux pastry cut in half, filled with Praline Creme Mousseline, studded with almonds on the shell, and then covered with a light dusting of powdered sugar.

Pain au Chocolat

Php 170 (per piece)

A dessert made with the same dough as croissants, but is generously filled with melted sticks of chocolate.

Beignets

Php 150 (for 12 pieces)

A type of fritter made of Choux pastry, shaped into pillows and dusted with powdered sugar.

Profiteroles

Php 210 (for 7 pieces)

Similar to cream puffs, but filled with ice cream instead. Profiteroles are another type of Choux dessert filled with ice cream then coated with a thin layer of chocolate ganache.

Eclair

Php 120 (per serving)

Another Choux dessert, but piped into a line rather than a dome. It is filled with diplomat cream and covered in a layer of chocolate ganache

Drinks

Hot Chocolate

Php 80

Thick and rich Viennese-style Hot Chocolate with whipped cream and sprinkled with chocolate shavings

Gluhwein

Php 100

A type of alcoholic beverage with red wine, infused with lemon zest, and flavored with star anise, cinnamon, and clove.

Einspannler

Php 90

A Viennese-style coffee composed of espresso and topped with copious amounts of whipped cream. It is then served in a tall glass, but don't stir it no matter how tempting it is!

Weiner Melange

Php 70

A Viennese coffee drink made of a blend of espresso shots, milk, and milk foam. It is pretty similar to a latte, but with a fancier name and chocolate sprinkled on top.

Affogato

Php 100

An Italian coffee drink comprising of coffee an a few scoops of plain vanilla gelato. It is then garnished with chocolate sprinkles.

Doppio

Php 70

A bitter double-shot espresso for the saddest of hearts.

Caffe Macchiato

Php 80

A shot of espresso delicately topped with a small amount of foamed milk.

Cafe Con Leche

Php 80

A latte, made with equal parts espresso and frothed milk. It is famous for its use in latte art.
